Monday was my last day at my job. I am a licensed esthetician and I had a job at a large hospital working in plastic surgery. I did mostly chemical peels and microdermabrasion for cosmetic patients and other employees at the hospital for $18 an hour. I hated it. The time spent with patients was mostly fine, although over the last few years I have had a harder time talking to people. The isolation and polarization of the pandemic has increased my anxiety and made it statistically less likely that I will have anything in common with strangers. It feels like I have a fundamentally different set of values and view of the world than many other people in this country. I've always felt "different" but this is something bigger.
